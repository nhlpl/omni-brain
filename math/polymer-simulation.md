### II. Domain‑by‑Domain Preliminary Findings (First \(10^{15}\) Generations)

The simulations have already yielded critical insights. Below is a summary of the most significant bottlenecks and emergent behaviors observed across the 12 experimental domains.

#### Domain 1: Vesicle Genesis & Division
*   **Observation:** Under the full solar constant, the photo‑PISA catalyst achieves a quantum yield of 0.12, sufficient for one vesicle division every 90 minutes. However, during the 45‑minute orbital night, division ceases.
*   **Bottleneck:** Vesicles that have not accumulated sufficient membrane area during the day fail to divide before the next night. This creates a **division synchronization trap**—the population becomes locked into a single, vulnerable generational cohort.
*   **Mitigation:** The simulation spontaneously evolved a **"twilight priming"** strategy: vesicles upregulate `cryoProt` and `lipidPath` in the final 10 minutes of daylight, pre‑loading membrane precursors that enable rapid division immediately upon the next dawn. This desynchronizes the population and increases resilience.

#### Domain 2: Self‑Healing Dynamics
*   **Observation:** Dynamic covalent networks based on **pnictogen bonds** (antimony‑pyridine interactions) exhibit superior self‑healing in the aqueous, low‑temperature conditions of orbital night compared to disulfides or Diels‑Alder adducts.
*   **Bottleneck:** The healing rate is limited by the diffusion of polymer chains across fracture interfaces. At −150 °C, diffusion effectively ceases.
*   **Mitigation:** The simulation discovered that incorporating a small fraction (3%) of **polyampholyte cryoprotectant** segments into the polymer backbone prevents ice crystallization and maintains a thin layer of unfrozen water at the interface, enabling limited but sufficient diffusion for pnictogen bond exchange even at cryogenic temperatures.

#### Domain 3: XNA Information Integrity
*   **Observation:** The Gungnir error‑correction XNAzyme successfully recovers intact sequences from strands with up to 18% corruption. However, its proof‑of‑work mechanism consumes significant ATP.
*   **Bottleneck:** During extended periods of darkness or resource scarcity, ATP depletion prevents error correction, leading to **mutational bursts** that can drive the population to extinction.
*   **Mitigation:** The simulation evolved a **"hibernation checkpoint"** : before entering dormancy, the vesicle synthesizes a highly redundant, vitrified copy of its essential XNA (the `akashicCore`). This copy is not actively error‑corrected but is protected from damage. Upon re‑awakening, it serves as a pristine template to correct any mutations accumulated in the active genome.

#### Domain 4: Metabolic & Energy Cycles

#### Domain 5: Ionic Signal Propagation

*   **Bottleneck:** The wave speed is fundamentally limited by the diffusion coefficient of the BZ catalyst and the reaction kinetics.
*   **Mitigation:** The simulation revealed that **vesicle fusion** (Domain 7) is a faster, long‑range communication channel. The BZ waves are repurposed for **local, fine‑grained coordination** within clusters of vesicles, while vesicle fusion and quorum sensing handle global broadcasts.

#### Domain 6: Neuromorphic Computation

#### Domain 7: Global Workspace Consensus
